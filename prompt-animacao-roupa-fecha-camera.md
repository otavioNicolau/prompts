## APPROACH SEQUENCE (MAIN ACTION — VERY IMPORTANT)

**Phase 1 — Gentle sway (0–2s):**
- Subject is stationary
- She gently and naturally sways the garment left and right — small, casual movements
- The sway is subtle and relaxed, like naturally showing the piece to someone
- Fabric moves with natural physics during the sway — weight, drape, flutter matching the material
- She maintains eye contact with the camera, calm and natural
- The sway lasts approximately 2 seconds before transitioning

**Phase 2 — Slow approach (2s–end):**
- She begins moving toward the camera at a very slow, deliberate pace
- The movement is a smooth glide forward — controlled, not a rushed walk
- The garment stays extended forward, filling progressively more of the frame
- Her face and body disappear entirely behind the garment as she advances
- The fabric expands outward from center until it reaches every edge of the frame
- The final frame is 100% fabric — no background, no model, no empty space visible

**Final frame (CRITICAL):**
- The garment from Image 2 physically covers the entire camera frame completely
- Every corner and edge of the frame is covered by the fabric
- The last visible detail before full coverage is the texture and surface of the piece
- No fade, no digital wipe — the garment covers the lens naturally through physical proximity
- The video ends with the frame fully closed by the fabric
