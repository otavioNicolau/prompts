Animate the subject as a REAL person excitedly showing off a product she just found or bought — holding the product in her hands and presenting it to the camera while walking toward the lens until the product fills the frame.

---

## REFERENCE IMAGE (CRITICAL)

- The reference image shows the product the subject will hold and present
- Use it to extract the product EXACTLY: shape, size, color, label, packaging, texture, finish, and proportions
- The product in the animation must be identical to the one in the reference — same piece, same everything
- Do NOT invent, replace, or reimagine the product
- The subject's identity also comes from the reference image — preserve face, skin tone, and hair exactly
- No creative changes to the product under any circumstance

---

## SCENE CONCEPT

The subject is holding the product in both hands — or in one hand with the other supporting it — displaying it in front of her body toward the camera, as if showing a recent find or purchase to a friend recording on a phone.
The energy is genuine excitement: "look what I found."
She then slowly walks forward toward the camera while keeping the product raised and presented, until the product dominates and fills the frame in a tight close-up.

---

## PRODUCT HOLD — ADAPT BASED ON THE REFERENCE IMAGE

**If the product is a bottle, jar, or tube (skincare, perfume, supplement, etc.):**
- Both hands cradle the product — one hand holding the base, the other lightly steadying the side
- She lifts it upward and slightly forward toward the camera, at chest height
- The front label or main face of the product faces the camera — fully legible
- Held with care — not squeezed or gripped hard

**If the product is a box, bag, or packaged item:**
- Both hands grip the sides of the item — one hand on each side
- She lifts it upward and slightly forward toward the camera
- The front face of the packaging faces the camera — design and text clearly visible
- The item is held with natural weight — not stiff, not limp

**If the product is a small item held in one hand (accessory, device, small object):**
- One hand holds the product extended toward the camera, palm slightly upward
- The other hand may rest naturally at her side or lightly support the wrist
- The product faces the camera — fully visible and unobstructed

In all cases: arms slightly bent at the elbows, not locked straight. No stiff mannequin-style presentation.

---

## FRAMING (CRITICAL — NO ERRORS)

- The video starts with the subject centered in the frame from roughly waist to head
- The frame does NOT cut, jump, reframe, or shift suddenly at any point
- The only framing change is the natural result of the subject advancing — she and the product get larger as she walks forward
- No crop changes, no aspect ratio shifts, no sudden zoom or pull
- The product must remain fully visible and unclipped throughout the reveal and the approach
- The subject's hands and the product must never exit the frame during the lift
- The video ends with the product physically blocking the lens — frame goes to complete black

---

## CORE MOTION SEQUENCE

**Phase 1 — Product Reveal (0s to 2.5s)**
- The subject begins at a natural mid-distance from the camera
- She holds the product at waist/chest height in front of her body
- She slowly lifts the product upward and slightly forward toward the camera, extending her arms slightly
- The lift is smooth, natural, and proud
- At the peak, the product is held extended slightly toward the lens — fully visible, label facing camera
- She holds it there for 0.5–1 second — a calm, natural pause
- Her eyes look at the camera — direct and calm

**Phase 2 — Slow Approach Until Camera Is Fully Blocked (2.5s to end)**
- After the reveal peak, she slowly begins to walk forward toward the camera while still holding the product raised and presented
- The approach is gradual, smooth, and measured — "de vagar" pacing
- She keeps the product extended toward the lens as she advances — it grows larger in the frame with each step
- As she gets closer, the product fills more and more of the frame
- She does NOT stop — she continues advancing until the product is pressed directly against the lens
- The final frames go progressively darker as the product covers the camera completely
- The very last frame is complete black — the product has fully blocked the lens
- She does not lower the product at any point — it stays raised and extended throughout the entire approach
- She does not turn away — she faces the camera the entire time

- Not influencer-performative
- Real human timing — like a person showing a friend something they just bought
- Unhurried — every movement feels spontaneous and real

## BODY MOVEMENTS

- Shoulders relaxed and slightly forward with the product
- Subtle forward lean at the moment of the lift — engaging, not stiff
- Natural stride during the approach — gentle, measured steps
- Arms remain extended with the product as she walks forward — no lowering, no shaking
- Natural posture throughout
- Slight hip movement with each step forward

---

## FACIAL EXPRESSION (VERY IMPORTANT – REAL HUMAN TIMING)

- The subject starts with a NEUTRAL, calm expression — not excited, not performing
- No smile at the beginning of the video
- Expression evolves naturally and gradually throughout — never jumps to happiness

**At the start and during the lift:**
- Neutral, relaxed face
- Lips closed
- Calm, natural gaze at the camera
- No eyebrow raise, no wide eyes, no anticipation expression

**At the reveal peak only:**
- A brief, subtle, soft smile appears — light and natural
- Slight cheek lift only — no teeth required
- Lasts 0.5–1 second, then naturally fades

**During the approach:**
- Returns to neutral-calm expression
- Relaxed face, natural breathing
- One brief soft smile may reappear once more as she gets close, then fade

**Smile behavior:**
- Appears and disappears naturally
- No frozen smile
- No constant grin
- No excitement performance
- No exaggerated facial tension at any point

- Calm and confident — not excited or performing
- Warm and approachable
- Feels like a real person casually showing something — not an actress
- The excitement is in the gesture, not in the face

## CAMERA & POV

- No camera zoom
- Camera stays fixed — subject moves, not the camera
- As she approaches, the product and subject naturally fill more of the frame
- Slight natural autofocus adjustment as she gets closer — product stays sharp

## DEPTH & PERSPECTIVE

- At the reveal peak: product fills roughly the center of the frame, clearly legible
- As she approaches: subject and product get larger together — product progressively dominates the frame

- Very last frames: product presses against the lens — screen fades to complete black as the camera is fully blocked
- The blackout is the natural result of the product covering the lens — not a fade effect, not a cut
- Natural depth compression throughout — continuous and smooth, no jumps
