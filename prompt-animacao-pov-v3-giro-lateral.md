## MOVEMENT SEQUENCE (FULL)

### Step 1 — Approach + clothing detail touch

- The subject starts at a natural distance from the camera
- She calmly takes two small, natural steps forward toward the camera
- While approaching, she gently touches and lightly holds a detail on the clothing (bow, lace, fabric texture, or waistline — whichever is most prominent in Image 1)
- The gesture is delicate, subtle, and realistic — like naturally showing a detail to someone
- No emphasis, no posing, no exaggeration
- After briefly touching the detail, she naturally releases it

### Step 2 — Step back to original position

- She takes one small step back to approximately her original position
- The movement is smooth and natural — not a rush
- She settles into a relaxed stance, weight slightly to one side

### Step 3 — Torso rotation to the side (MAIN ADDITION)

- From her standing position, she slowly and naturally rotates her torso to one side
- The rotation is approximately 60–90 degrees — clear side profile, NOT a full 180° or 360°
- TORSO ROTATION ONLY — feet stay in place, no walking or spinning
- The rotation is slow and deliberate — casual, not runway
- Arms remain in a relaxed natural position during the turn — one hand may rest lightly on the hip
- As she turns, the side profile of the clothing becomes fully visible:
  - Waistline fit from the side
  - Hip and torso silhouette
  - Fabric drape and length from a side angle
  - Any back or side details of the outfit

### Step 4 — Hold the side + glance back

- She holds the side position naturally for approximately 1–1.5 seconds
- Slight natural weight shift or sway — she does not freeze completely
- She turns her head naturally back toward the camera — a casual sideways glance
- Expression during this glance: soft, natural, relaxed

### Step 5 — Return to front

- She slowly and naturally rotates her torso back to face the camera
- The movement is as smooth and relaxed as the turn out
- She settles back into a comfortable forward-facing stance
- Brief natural pause at the end — she holds position for the camera
